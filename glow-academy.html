🔄 SECTION 2: SCRUM FRAMEWORK DOCUMENTATION

Scrum planning documentation, epics, user stories, roadmap, and sprint scope for GlowUp mobile app.

Product Framing

The GlowUp app is not a salon booking app. It is a masterclass marketplace for township beauty entrepreneurs — helping informal hairdressers, nail techs, and barbers turn skills into income by teaching, verifying, and connecting learners to opportunity.

Why This Works

  • ✓ Fits the brief — solves a real problem in the informal economy
  • ✓ Commercially viable through class listings, premium profiles, verification, and lead generation
  • ✓ More original than a simple booking app — focus is masterclasses + skill proof + opportunity

Product Angle — Creator-Trainer Marketplace

  • Hairdressers can run braiding, wig-install, and styling masterclasses
  • Nail techs can run manicure, acrylic, and nail art masterclasses
  • Barbers can run fade, beard, and clipper masterclasses

📌 Core Problem Statement

"Informal beauty professionals can be highly skilled, but they struggle to prove credibility, package their knowledge, and turn their skills into income beyond walk-in services."

💡 Core Idea

  • A trainer creates a masterclass
  • Learners book and pay
  • After completion, learners get a digital badge or certificate
  • Trainers can also promote services or sell starter kits
  • The app helps turn one class into future income

📋 USER STORIES

Epic 1: Trainer Profile and Verification
  • As a trainer, I want to create a profile with my photo, skills, and location so that learners can trust my masterclass listing.
  • As a trainer, I want to upload before-and-after photos of my work so that I can show proof of my skill.
  • As an admin, I want to verify trainer profiles so that the app stays credible and safe.
Epic 2: Masterclass Listings and Discovery
  • As a trainer, I want to create a masterclass listing with a title, date, price, and venue so that learners can see my class details.
  • As a learner, I want to search for masterclasses near me so that I can find affordable training in my area.
  • As a learner, I want to filter classes by hair, nails, or barbering so that I can choose the right skill category.
Epic 3: Booking and Payments
  • As a learner, I want to book a seat in a masterclass so that I can secure my spot.
  • As a trainer, I want to see a list of booked learners so that I can plan for class size.
  • As a learner, I want to pay for the class in the app so that I do not need to handle cash manually.
Epic 4: Learning Progress and Certificates
  • As a learner, I want to receive a digital badge after completing a class so that I can prove I attended.
  • As a trainer, I want to mark a learner as completed so that the app can generate a certificate.
  • As a learner, I want to view my training history so that I can track my growth over time.
Epic 5: Community and Opportunity Matching
  • As a learner, I want to find practice models so that I can improve my skills.
  • As a trainer, I want to post opportunities for assistants or models so that my students can get real experience.
  • As a learner, I want to be suggested for local beauty jobs so that I can start earning after training.

🏃 SPRINT 1 SCOPE

For the hackathon, Sprint 1 is kept small and realistic. The scope covers:

  • ✅ Create trainer profile
  • ✅ Create masterclass listing
  • ✅ Browse masterclasses
  • ✅ Book a class

This provides a clear first slice of value without overbuilding, and keeps the sprint focused for good Scrum execution.

👥 SECTION 3: USER PERSONAS

Three core user personas representing key roles in the township beauty ecosystem.

PERSONA 1: BUKELWA M
Township Nail Technician and Masterclass Trainer
📍 Age: 28  |  📍 Location: Gugulethu, Cape Town  |  💼 Business: Works from home and shared beauty space

Background: Bukelwa is a self-taught nail technician who built her client base through social media, word of mouth, and consistent client work. She now runs small masterclasses alongside normal client appointments, but still manages bookings, payments, and class coordination manually.

Goals: Grow income by teaching masterclasses, build stronger reputation as professional trainer, attract more students, eventually rent or own permanent space.

Pain Points: Struggles to organise bookings for both clients and students, finds it hard to prove credibility beyond social media posts, needs better way to manage cash and digital payments.

Needs: Simple way to list masterclasses, platform to show work and experience, booking support and payment tracking, growth from informal to stable business.

PERSONA 2: AYANDA DLAMINI
Learner / Aspiring Beauty Professional
🎓 Age: 19  |  📍 Location: Kuils River, Cape Town  |  📚 Role: Student and part-time beauty learner

Background: Ayanda is interested in learning nail art and hair styling so she can earn money while still studying. She looks at social media to find beauty trainers, but doesn't always know who is trustworthy or which classes are worth the money.

Goals: Find affordable and trustworthy masterclasses nearby, learn a skill to make money, receive proof of completion, build confidence with paying clients.

Pain Points: Users struggle to identify credible trainers, compare class pricing and inclusions, and access clear class information, making it difficult to feel confident that a booking is worth the cost.

PERSONA 3: OKUHLE JACK
Venue Partner / Community Space Owner
🏢 Age: 41  |  📍 Location: Langa, Cape Town  |  🏠 Business Type: Community Venue Owner

Background: Okuhle owns a small venue that can be rented out for beauty classes and training sessions. She wants to support local entrepreneurs while generating additional income through organised and reliable venue bookings.

Goals: Rent out her space to trainers and students, earn extra income, support local businesses, and manage bookings efficiently.

Pain Points: Venue owners struggle to advertise available spaces, manage booking schedules, communicate rental information clearly, and verify the trustworthiness of trainers before sharing their facilities.
